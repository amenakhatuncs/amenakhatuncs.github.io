About Me

I am a Research Scientist in the Quantum Systems team at CSIRO’s Data61, leading innovative research in quantum artificial intelligence. My work addresses high-impact challenges in defence, healthcare, cybersecurity, and critical infrastructure. With a PhD in Deep Learning from Queensland University of Technology, I specialize in quantum transfer learning, generative models, and adversarially robust quantum machine learning. Passionate about ethical and secure AI, I contribute to global research communities, mentor emerging talent, and advocate for diversity in STEM.

Research

My research integrates quantum computing with machine learning to develop efficient, secure, and scalable quantum AI systems:

  • Healthcare: Developed quantum generative models producing high-fidelity medical images with 2,000x fewer parameters, preserving patient privacy. Collaborated with MITRE and Cleveland Clinic for disease diagnosis and outcome prediction.
  • Cybersecurity: Lead quantum adversarial machine learning research, creating defence strategies to purify adversarial noise, enhancing model robustness for applications like autonomous vehicles and healthcare.
  • Transport: Developing quantum optimization for pedestrian and accessibility planning for the Brisbane 2032 Olympics, funded by Queensland Government.
