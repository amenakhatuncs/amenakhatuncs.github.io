About me

I am a Research Scientist in the Quantum Systems team at CSIRO’s Data61, where I lead research in quantum artificial intelligence to address high-impact challenges across defence and intelligence, healthcare, cybersecurity, and critical infrastructure. With a strong foundation in traditional AI and deep expertise in next-generation quantum AI, I am passionate about building intelligent systems that are ethical, secure, and future-ready. My mission is to advance quantum AI technologies that serve society, inspire innovation, and build a more secure and inclusive future.

Research

My research blends quantum computing with machine learning to create powerful quantum AI systems that are efficient, secure, and scalable. In healthcare, I’ve developed quantum generative learning models that generate high-fidelity medical images using 2,000x fewer parameters than classical models, in collaboration with MITRE and Cleveland Clinic. In cybersecurity, I lead research on quantum adversarial machine learning, developing defence strategies to purify adversarial noise. I am also working on quantum optimisation techniques for pedestrian movement and accessibility planning for the Brisbane 2032 Olympic and Paralympic Games, in collaboration with Griffith University.
